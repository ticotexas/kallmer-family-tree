# Obituary of Mrs. Caroline Kallmer

## Longest Continuous Resident Passes Away in Colorado

Mrs. Caroline Kallmer, who is credited with the honor and distinction of having maintained a continuous residence in Odebolt longer than any other person, died at five o'clock last Friday evening, October 27, at the home of her daughter in Sterling, Colorado. Mention of her illness was made in these columns last week, but for a few days prior to her death she was supposed to be gaining and plans were made to bring her home Monday. Friday she began to sink again and passed away quietly at the hour mentioned. Death was due to intestinal trouble and the general decline incident to old age. 

The body was at once prepared for shipment to Odebolt and reached here Monday noon. Funeral services were held Sunday morning in Sterling and a short service here Tuesday afternoon, conducted by Rev. Robert McInturff, following which the remains were laid to rest in the local cemetery. Those familiar with the early history of the town give her the honor of being the town's oldest continuous resident. She came here in 1877 when only four buildings marked the site of the present handsome city and it was her constant home until death.

## Early Life and Family History

Caroline Jacobson was born in Schleswig, Germany, June 17, 1832, and was thus 79 years, 4 months and 10 days old when death overtook her. In 1856 she was married to Jacob Kallmer, both then residents of the native country. In 1872 they forsook the land of their birth and came to Chicago. Five years later Odebolt claimed her as a resident and during the succeeding thirty-four years she was closely identified with the town's progress. 

Six daughters and one son were born to the couple. One daughter, Mrs. Lena Beck, died several years ago and another daughter passed away at the age of nine years. The surviving children, with their father, are:
* Mrs. I.M. Iverson of Odebolt
* Andrew C. Kallmer of Odebolt
* Mrs. C.C. Perry of Houston, Texas
* Mrs. Henry Warneke of Denver
* Mrs. Herman Jacobe of Sterling, Colorado

She is also survived by two sisters, Mrs. J.C. Hartwick of Aberdeen, So. Dak., and Mrs. William Rousch of Sterling, Colorado, and by one brother, Broder Jacobson, of Galva.

## A Legacy of Friendship

Deceased was a good mother, a good woman and had a legion of friends to whom her death comes as a great shock. We voice the sentiments of the people of this community when we extend tenderest tokens of sympathy to the surviving relatives.
