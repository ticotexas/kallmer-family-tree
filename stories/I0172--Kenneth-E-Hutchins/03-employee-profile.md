# Kenneth Hutchins at Weston & Tinsley

Kenneth ("Ken") Hutchins joined W&T on **July 3, 1944**, as an Instrument Assembler.

He began his career in the company's **Special Products Division** in the Model Shop, where wartime apparatus was being produced during World War II. His work specialized in **engraving and assembly**.

In **1951**, Ken transferred to the **Instrument Department**, where he continued assembling and performing the final ranging of the company's instruments.

Born and educated in **Pennsylvania**, Ken later moved to **Bloomfield, New Jersey**, where he and his wife made their home. At the time this employee profile was published, they had a married daughter, and Ken had become a grandfather.

Outside of work, Ken enjoyed installing television antennas, taking automobile trips with his family, and hunting deer.

*This biography is adapted from a W&T employee profile published in the company's 25th Anniversary commemorative booklet, circa 1969.*
