# Obituary of Rev. Vernon Huffey

Reverend Vernon Huffey, age 88, of Storm Lake, died Monday, March 27, 2006 at the Buena Vista Regional Medical Center in Storm Lake.

Funeral services were held on Friday, March 31, 2006, at 11:00 a.m. at Faith United Assembly of God Church in Storm Lake. Burial was in the Storm Lake Cemetery. The Sliefert Funeral Home of Storm Lake is in charge of the arrangements.

Friends who wish to send on-line condolences to the family or view Reverend Huffey's memorial folder online may do so via the Sliefert Funeral Home website: www.sliefert.com

## Early Life and Education

Vernon L. Huffey was born on September 9, 1917 in Postville, to Walter A. and Tilda B. (Olson) Huffey. He lived with his parents in both Postville and Waterville. Vernon attended Dalby Country School in Allamakee County, then Waterville High School from 1931–1932, graduating in 1935 from Decorah High School in Decorah. 

Following high school, Vernon attended Central Bible College in Springfield, Missouri in 1938 and North Central Bible College in Minneapolis, Minnesota from 1938–1940, where he graduated after completing a three year course in two and a half years. In 1953, Vernon received his B.A. degree from Buena Vista College in Storm Lake, Iowa, graduating Cum Laude. He continued his graduate studies at Drake University in Des Moines, Iowa from 1953–1954.

## Marriage and Family

On March 31, 1941, Vernon was united in marriage to Marian E. Lepa at the Assembly of God Church in Decorah, Iowa. To this union, two children were born, La Don and Paul.

## Ministry and Community Service

Vernon was an active member of many Assembly of God churches over the years. He was Presbyter, 30 years, Iowa District of the Assemblies of God, comprehensive oversight of Assembly of God Churches in Sections 1, 2 and 3 comprising all of northern Iowa. He was a member of nine member Board of Directors, administering church business in Iowa and northern Missouri. In addition, Vernon served 30 years on the Credentials Committee examining clergymen to receive license to preach and ordination.

Over the years, Vernon was a minister for the Assembly of God Churches in Oelwein, Iowa, Conrad, Iowa, Waukon, Iowa and Storm Lake, Iowa. He retired from the active ministry in January of 1982. Then from 1989–1997, Vernon was a pastor at Carriage Manor Chapel in Mesa, Arizona.

Additionally, Vernon was very active in the communities he served. He was a life-time member of the Veteran's of Foreign Wars, past-president of the Buena Vista College Alumni Association and past-president of the Buena Vista College Honor Society.

Vernon loved his family, loved God, and enjoyed the many beauties of the earth in gardening. He was tremendous as a husband, father, grandfather, pastor and friend. He'll be sorely missed and never replaced!

## Military Service

After entering the service on June 13, 1945, Vernon served as chaplain in the U.S. Army, crossing the Atlantic and Pacific oceans thirty-six times. He was honorably discharged on March 31, 1948. From 1948–1974, Vernon's service continued as he served in the United States Army Ready Reserve. Vernon attained the rank of Colonel in the U.S. Army as well as receiving a WWII Victory Medal and a Meritorious Service Medal.
